Western Monuments

Discover the most iconic historical monuments from the western regions of the world.

Statue of Liberty

New York, USA

A colossal neoclassical sculpture on Liberty Island in New York Harbor, a gift from France representing freedom and democracy.

Dedicated: 1886
Height: 93m (305 ft)
350 pieces shipped from France

Chichen Itza

Yucatan, Mexico

A large pre-Columbian city built by the Maya people, featuring the iconic El Castillo step pyramid.

Built: 600-900 CE
Precise astronomical alignments
Population: 50,000 at peak

Mount Rushmore

South Dakota, USA

A massive sculpture carved into the granite face of Mount Rushmore featuring 60-foot heads of four U.S. presidents.

Carved: 1927-1941
90% carved with dynamite
Height: 1,745m (5,725 ft)

Roman Colosseum

Rome, Italy

The largest ancient amphitheater ever built, used for gladiatorial contests and public spectacles in ancient Rome.

Built: 70-80 CE
Capacity: 50,000-80,000
Largest amphitheater ever built

Eiffel Tower

Paris, France

A wrought-iron lattice tower on the Champ de Mars in Paris, named after engineer Gustave Eiffel whose company designed and built it.

Built: 1887-1889
Height: 330m (1,083 ft)
Struck by lightning 5-10 times yearly

Stonehenge

Wiltshire, England

A prehistoric monument consisting of a ring of standing stones, aligned with the sunrise at the summer solstice.

Built: 3000-2000 BCE
Astronomical alignments
Purpose remains mysterious

Machu Picchu

Peru

A 15th-century Inca citadel located in the Eastern Cordillera of southern Peru, often called the "Lost City of the Incas".

Built: c. 1450
Elevation: 2,430m (7,970 ft)
Abandoned during Spanish conquest
