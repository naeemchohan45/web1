Southern Monuments

Discover the most iconic historical monuments from the southern regions of the world.

Machu Picchu

Peru

A 15th-century Inca citadel located in the Eastern Cordillera of southern Peru, often called the "Lost City of the Incas".

Built: c. 1450
Elevation: 2,430m (7,970 ft)
Abandoned during Spanish conquest

Christ the Redeemer

Rio de Janeiro, Brazil

An Art Deco statue of Jesus Christ in Rio de Janeiro, standing 30 meters tall on top of the 700-meter Corcovado mountain.

Built: 1922-1931
Height: 30m (98 ft)
Struck by lightning multiple times

Easter Island Moai

Easter Island, Chile

Monolithic human figures carved by the Rapa Nui people between 1250 and 1500 CE, representing deified ancestors.

Built: 1250-1500 CE
Average weight: 14 tons
Transport method remains debated

Sydney Opera House

Sydney, Australia

A multi-venue performing arts center famous for its distinctive shell-like design, recognized as a masterpiece of modern architecture.

Built: 1959-1973
Roof: 1,056,006 tiles
UNESCO World Heritage Site

Angkor Wat

Siem Reap, Cambodia

The largest religious monument in the world, originally constructed as a Hindu temple dedicated to Vishnu, later transforming into a Buddhist temple.

Built: 12th century
Area: 162.6 hectares
Largest religious structure

Great Zimbabwe

Zimbabwe

A medieval city in the south-eastern hills of Zimbabwe, built by the Bantu civilization between the 11th and 15th centuries.

Built: 11th-15th century
Covered 7.22 km² at peak
Population: 18,000 at peak

Petra

Jordan

An ancient Nabataean city famous for its rock-cut architecture and water conduit system, known as the "Rose City" for the color of its stone.

Established: 5th century BCE
Al-Khazneh is most famous structure
Advanced water management system
