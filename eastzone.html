Eastern Monuments

Discover the most iconic historical monuments from the eastern regions of the world.

Great Wall of China

China

The longest wall in the world, stretching over 21,000 km, built over centuries to protect Chinese states from invasions.

Built: 7th century BCE onwards
Length: 21,196 km (13,171 mi)
Construction lasted 2,000+ years

Taj Mahal

Agra, India

An ivory-white marble mausoleum commissioned in 1632 by Shah Jahan to house the tomb of his favorite wife, Mumtaz Mahal.

Built: 1632-1653
Workers: 20,000 artisans
Materials from across Asia

Angkor Wat

Siem Reap, Cambodia

The largest religious monument in the world, originally constructed as a Hindu temple dedicated to Vishnu, later transforming into a Buddhist temple.

Built: 12th century
Area: 162.6 hectares
Largest religious structure

Borobudur

Java, Indonesia

The world's largest Buddhist temple, featuring nine stacked platforms, six square and three circular, topped by a central dome.

Built: 9th century
504 Buddha statues
Built as a giant stupa

Potala Palace

Lhasa, Tibet

The winter palace of the Dalai Lama since the 7th century, standing at 3,700 meters above sea level on Marpo Ri hill.

Built: 1645-1694
13 stories, 1,000 rooms
Height: 117m (384 ft)

Petra

Jordan

An ancient Nabataean city famous for its rock-cut architecture and water conduit system, known as the "Rose City" for the color of its stone.

Established: 5th century BCE
Al-Khazneh is most famous structure
Advanced water management system

Himeji Castle

Himeji, Japan

A hilltop Japanese castle complex comprising 83 buildings with advanced defensive systems from the feudal period.

Built: 1333-1346
Known as "White Heron Castle"
Never destroyed by war or earthquake
