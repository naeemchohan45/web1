Marvels of Human Achievement

The Seven Wonders of the World

Explore All Categories of Wonders

Discover the most extraordinary constructions from different eras and natural formations that continue to inspire awe.

Great Pyramid of Giza

Giza, Egypt

The oldest and only surviving wonder of the ancient world, built as a tomb for Pharaoh Khufu around 2560 BCE.

Built: ~2560 BCE
Height: 146.6m (481 ft)
Age: ~4,500 years

Hanging Gardens of Babylon

Babylon (Iraq)

A legendary terraced garden complex said to have been built by King Nebuchadnezzar II for his homesick wife.

Built: ~600 BCE
Marvel of irrigation engineering
Exact location unknown

Statue of Zeus at Olympia

Olympia, Greece

A giant seated figure of Zeus, about 13m tall, made by the Greek sculptor Phidias around 435 BCE.

Built: ~435 BCE
Made of ivory and gold plates
Destroyed in 5th century CE

Temple of Artemis

Ephesus (Turkey)

A Greek temple dedicated to the goddess Artemis, rebuilt three times before its final destruction in 401 CE.

First built: ~550 BCE
127 marble columns
Destroyed by Herostratus in 356 BCE

Mausoleum at Halicarnassus

Bodrum, Turkey

A tomb built between 353-350 BCE for Mausolus, a satrap in the Persian Empire, by his wife Artemisia.

Built: 353-350 BCE
Origin of the word "mausoleum"
Stood for 16 centuries

Colossus of Rhodes

Rhodes, Greece

A giant statue of the sun god Helios, erected to celebrate Rhodes' victory over Cyprus in 305 BCE.

Built: 292-280 BCE
Height: ~33m (108 ft)
Destroyed in 226 BCE earthquake

Lighthouse of Alexandria

Alexandria, Egypt

A lighthouse built by the Ptolemaic Kingdom between 280-247 BCE, one of the tallest man-made structures for many centuries.

Built: 280-247 BCE
First lighthouse in the world
Height: ~100m (330 ft)

Great Wall of China

China

A series of fortifications made of stone, brick, tamped earth, wood, and other materials, built along China's historical northern borders.

Built: 7th century BCE onward
Length: 21,196 km (13,171 mi)
Built for protection from invasions

Petra

Jordan

The "Rose City" carved directly into vibrant red sandstone cliffs, was the capital of the Nabatean Kingdom around 300 BCE.

Established: ~300 BCE
Carved into sandstone cliffs
Advanced water system

Christ the Redeemer

Rio de Janeiro, Brazil

An Art Deco statue of Jesus Christ created by French sculptor Paul Landowski and built by Brazilian engineer Heitor da Silva Costa.

Completed: 1931
Height: 30m (98 ft)
Symbol of Christianity worldwide

Machu Picchu

Cusco Region, Peru

A 15th-century Inca citadel situated on a mountain ridge 2,430 meters above sea level, often called the "Lost City of the Incas".

Built: ~1450 CE
Elevation: 2,430m (7,970 ft)
Aligns with astronomical events

Chichen Itza

Yucatán, Mexico

A large pre-Columbian city built by the Maya people, featuring the iconic stepped pyramid El Castillo (Temple of Kukulcan).

Built: ~600-900 CE
Astronomical alignments
91 steps on each side (total 364 + platform = 365)

Roman Colosseum

Rome, Italy

An oval amphitheater in the center of Rome, used for gladiatorial contests and public spectacles in ancient times.

Built: 70-80 CE
Capacity: 50,000-80,000
Largest amphitheater ever built

Taj Mahal

Agra, India

An ivory-white marble mausoleum commissioned in 1632 by the Mughal emperor Shah Jahan to house the tomb of his favorite wife, Mumtaz Mahal.

Built: 1632-1653
20,000 artisans worked on it
Made of white marble with precious stone inlays

Grand Canyon

Arizona, USA

A steep-sided canyon carved by the Colorado River, revealing layers of rock that date back 2 billion years.

Length: 446 km (277 mi)
Depth: 1.8 km (1.1 mi)
Formed over 5-6 million years

Great Barrier Reef

Coral Sea, Australia

The world's largest coral reef system composed of over 2,900 individual reefs and 900 islands stretching over 2,300 km.

Length: 2,300 km (1,400 mi)
Home to 1,500+ fish species
Visible from space

Aurora Borealis

Arctic Regions

Natural light display in Earth's sky, predominantly seen in high-latitude regions around the Arctic and Antarctic.

Caused by solar wind particles
Colors: green, pink, red, yellow, blue
Best seen Sept-April

Mount Everest

Nepal/China border

Earth's highest mountain above sea level, located in the Mahalangur Himal sub-range of the Himalayas.

Height: 8,848.86 m (29,032 ft)
Grows ~4mm per year
First climbed: 1953

Victoria Falls

Zambia/Zimbabwe

A waterfall on the Zambezi River at the border between Zambia and Zimbabwe, known for being the world's largest sheet of falling water.

Width: 1,708 m (5,604 ft)
Height: 108 m (354 ft)
Local name: "The Smoke That Thunders"

Parícutin Volcano

Michoacán, Mexico

A cinder cone volcano that suddenly appeared in a cornfield in 1943 and grew to 424 meters in 8 years.

Appeared: February 20, 1943
Final height: 424 m (1,391 ft)
Last erupted: 1952

Harbor of Rio de Janeiro

Rio de Janeiro, Brazil

A stunning natural bay surrounded by dramatic granite mountains including Sugarloaf Mountain and Corcovado Peak.

One of the world's largest bays
Surrounded by granite peaks
Discovered by Portuguese in 1502
