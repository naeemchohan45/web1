Northern Monuments

Discover the most iconic historical monuments from the northern regions of the world.

Great Wall of China

China

The longest wall in the world, stretching over 21,000 km, built over centuries to protect Chinese states from invasions.

Built: 7th century BCE onwards
Length: 21,196 km (13,171 mi)
Construction lasted 2,000+ years

Roman Colosseum

Rome, Italy

The largest ancient amphitheater ever built, used for gladiatorial contests and public spectacles in ancient Rome.

Built: 70-80 CE
Capacity: 50,000-80,000
Largest amphitheater ever built

Stonehenge

Wiltshire, England

A prehistoric monument consisting of a ring of standing stones, aligned with the sunrise at the summer solstice.

Built: 3000-2000 BCE
Astronomical alignments
Purpose remains mysterious

Moscow Kremlin

Moscow, Russia

A fortified complex at the heart of Moscow, overlooking the Moskva River, serving as the official residence of the Russian president.

Built: 1482-1495
Area: 27.7 hectares
Contains 5 palaces and 4 cathedrals

Acropolis of Athens

Athens, Greece

An ancient citadel located on a rocky outcrop above Athens, containing the remains of several ancient buildings including the Parthenon.

Built: 5th century BCE
Most famous: Parthenon temple
Elevation: 150m (490 ft)

Eiffel Tower

Paris, France

A wrought-iron lattice tower on the Champ de Mars in Paris, named after engineer Gustave Eiffel whose company designed and built it.

Built: 1887-1889
Height: 330m (1,083 ft)
Struck by lightning 5-10 times yearly

Forbidden City

Beijing, China

The imperial palace complex from the Ming dynasty to the end of the Qing dynasty, now housing the Palace Museum.

Built: 1406-1420
Size: 720,000 sq m (180 acres)
9,999 rooms (traditionally)
